five novels in which fielding appropriates and revises a misogynist satiric tradition to allow it to intersect with the developing discourse of sensibility she offers not only fresh interpretations of masculinity but also a new conception of women as strong and educated able to see the world from a satirical perspective and critique it from a position of virtue constrained by but not limited to
