nbc television this research project uses the case study method to explore nbcs 1983-84 television season dubbed the september train wreck of 1983 examining trade publication articles mainstream press and the programs themselves this case study applies a network executive auteur theory to the programming strategies of grant tinker nbc chairman and ceo and brandon tartikoff president of nbc entertainment conventional wisdom at the time portrayed tinker and tartikoffs network decisions as desperate and unfocused however a closer look suggests that tinker and tartikoff essentially invented a hybrid of highbrowlowbrowmiddlebrow programming to combat the audience fragmentation brought on by the penetration of cable television
