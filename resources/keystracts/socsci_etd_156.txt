urban land use the traditional theories of uran land use were promulgated in social economic and political environments markedly different from those of today  the then-existent social philosophy was that of laissez-faire  collective action particularly on the part of government to control land use was minimalmore recently however numberous kinds of collective action have come to play an important role in the determination of urban land usethe changed circumstances under which urban land use is contolled and determined raises questions as to the operational adequacy and empirical relevancy of the traditional theories of urban land use
